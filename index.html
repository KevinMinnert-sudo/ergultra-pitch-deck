Why ErgUltra exists

It started with a training partner and a crazy idea.

I trained with Oli Eman — friend, training partner, Ironman finisher and Backyard Ultra runner. In a Backyard Ultra you run, in last-man-standing format, one loop of roughly 6.7 km every hour, again and again. Miss a round and you're out. Exactly one winner remains.

“We carried this format over to the Concept2 in training — round after round, until only one is left. Then I wanted an app for it. There was none.”

That session turned into a mission. I researched, worked through podcasts and YouTube, dug into the sport science and the tech — and built ErgUltra. Because to this day there is no solution for it on the market.

The hardware · context for non-erg readers

Three machines. One on-board computer.

Concept2 builds the world's most-used ergometers (“ergs” — training machines that precisely measure every watt). All three share the same on-board computer, the PM5 (Performance Monitor 5), which ErgUltra programs and reads live over Bluetooth. The Backyard Ultra rotation means switching between them, round after round.

Row
RowErg · rowing
The gold standard of indoor rowing. A full-body, low-impact stroke that drives legs, core, back and arms in one movement.
Full body · legs-led
Ski
SkiErg · Nordic skiing
The double-poling motion of cross-country skiing. Upper-body and core dominant, with a high cardio demand.
Upper body · core
Bike
BikeErg · cycling
An air-resistance bike with the same flywheel feel. Leg-dominant power in a smooth, responsive ride.
Legs · lower body

Product images © Concept2 — shown for identification.

Why this matters for ErgUltra: one BLE/PM5 protocol drives all three, so a single app covers every discipline — and the multi-erg rotation (Ski → Row → Bike) is a format no competitor offers.

Product logic05
Ecosystem & data portability

We meet athletes where their data already lives.

ErgUltra is not a walled garden. Two integrations connect it to the athlete's existing world — one for reach, one for credibility. Both are implemented today via OAuth (Supabase Edge Functions, tokens stored server-side).

Strava

Reach & social distribution

Why it matters: Strava is where endurance athletes already keep their training life. Every erg session pushed there reaches an audience far beyond the erg niche.

Strategy

Acquisition engine — each shared workout is organic reach and social proof inside athletes' existing networks, feeding the See → Train → Share loop at near-zero acquisition cost.

User benefit

One training log for everything. Kudos and visibility from the whole athletic circle — no manual double-entry.

Official record & trust

Why it matters: The Concept2 Online Logbook is the sport's system of record. Syncing to it makes ErgUltra a first-class citizen in the Concept2 world, not a silo.

Strategy

Credibility & retention — results flow into official Concept2 challenges and rankings; open data portability lowers switching risk and positions ErgUltra as the hub, not a lock-in.

User benefit

Sessions count toward official Concept2 challenges and season rankings, with a verified, portable history the athlete always owns.

Integrations are strategy, not plumbing: Strava drives low-cost acquisition and viral reach; the Concept2 Logbook delivers credibility and keeps athletes inside the ecosystem. Together they make ErgUltra the central hub of an erg athlete's data — with sharing tracked as a core conversion event (PostHog share).

Architecture12
The technical moat

PM5 BLE & CSAFE — this is where the real value sits.

The app is master, the PM5 is slave: ErgUltra programs workouts directly onto the on-board computer and receives real-time data (~every 500 ms). This device-near stack can't be copied in weeks.

Multi-erg
Up to 3 PM5s at once
SkiErg · RowErg · BikeErg connected simultaneously for the rotation.
  • CSAFE protocol — frames, byte-stuffing, command builders based on the open Concept2 spec.
  • Workout programming — distance, countdown, native FIXEDTIME intervals directly on the PM5.
  • Live data — distance, pace, watts, stroke rate, HR, per-stroke force curves.
  • Auto-reconnect — exponential backoff, health checks, characteristic caching.
  • “Wait for first stroke” — timer sync straight from the device's BLE data.
Live Solo Ultra session — metrics rendered in real time straight from the PM5

Principle “Custom stack = moat”: any simplification of this BLE/CSAFE/PM5 path would destroy the differentiation. This is exactly where the external review must be most careful.
